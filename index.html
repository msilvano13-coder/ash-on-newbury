What is IBE?

Invisible Bead
Extensions

Invisible Bead Extensions (IBE) is the most innovative and gentle hair extension method on the market. Unlike traditional extensions that use heat, glue, or braiding, IBE uses tiny, medical-grade beads to attach individual wefts of hair — working with your natural hair's growth pattern, not against it.

The result is hair that moves naturally, feels weightless, and causes zero damage to your existing hair. With proper maintenance, IBE extensions last months longer than other methods and can be adjusted as your hair grows.

At Ash on Newbury, we were among the first salons in Boston to become IBE certified — and our mastery of the technique has made us the area's go-to destination for those who want the very best.

No Heat. No Glue. No Damage.

IBE uses only medical-grade silicone-lined beads — nothing that compromises the integrity of your natural hair.

Completely Undetectable

Applied in rows that lay flat against the head, IBE extensions are invisible even in updos and ponytails.

Long-Lasting Results

With routine maintenance appointments, IBE extensions can last 6–12 months and beyond.

Custom Color Matched

Every weft is hand-selected and color-matched to blend seamlessly with your natural hair.

Common Questions

IBE Extensions
FAQ

What are IBE (Invisible Bead Extensions)?+
IBE stands for Invisible Bead Extensions — a hair extension method that uses tiny, medical-grade silicone-lined beads to attach hand-tied wefts to your natural hair. Unlike tape-ins, sew-ins, or fusion extensions, IBE uses no heat, no glue, and no braiding, making it the most gentle and damage-free extension method available. The beads are virtually undetectable, even in updos and ponytails.
How long do IBE extensions last?+
With proper care, IBE extensions last 6 to 12 months or longer. They require a move-up appointment every 6 to 8 weeks, where the beads are repositioned closer to the root as your natural hair grows. The wefts themselves can be reused across multiple move-ups, making IBE a great long-term investment.
Do IBE extensions damage your natural hair?+
No. IBE is considered the least damaging extension method available. The medical-grade silicone-lined beads grip the hair gently without causing breakage or tension alopecia. No heat, glue, or chemicals are used at any point. When installed by a certified stylist like those at Ash on Newbury, IBE extensions allow your natural hair to grow healthily underneath.
Where can I get IBE extensions in Boston?+
Ash on Newbury at 134 Newbury Street in Boston's Back Bay is the area's premier IBE extension studio. They were among the first salons in Boston to become IBE certified, and every stylist on the team is a trained IBE specialist. Appointments are booked through Vagaro, and consultations are always complimentary.
What is an IBE move-up?+
A move-up is a maintenance appointment scheduled every 6 to 8 weeks. Your stylist removes the beads, repositions the wefts closer to your roots to account for natural hair growth, and reattaches them. This keeps your extensions seamless and comfortable. Move-ups are essential to the longevity of IBE extensions.
Why is IBE better than tape-in or fusion extensions?+
IBE is widely considered the best extension method because it uses no heat, adhesives, or braiding. Tape-ins can slip and damage hair with adhesive residue. Keratin fusion bonds use heat that weakens hair over time. Micro-links can cause tension and breakage. IBE's bead-and-weft system distributes weight evenly, lays completely flat, and is ideal even for fine or thin hair.
